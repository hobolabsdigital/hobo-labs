## The Problem
Visualizing the abstract scale of environmental action can be difficult, making it hard for individuals to feel their contribution matters in fighting overfishing.

## The Solution
Architected an interactive WebGL experience where users design a low-poly fish with a personal message, linking their engagement to a live, massive virtual ocean synchronized across all users.

## Overview & Impact
Engineered the performant Three.js underwater world to render massive swarms of low-poly fish with vibrant colors and natural movements. Built a robust Python and Node.js backend pipeline that continuously scraped Oceana hashtags in real-time and synchronized new entries via AWS cloud solutions to update the client-side swarm instantly. The accessible low-poly aesthetics created an emotional connection, making the serious topic of overfishing tangible and inviting without feeling explicitly technical.
